3. Detailed Crosswalk

Group A: Direct Student Services Use Cases

#8 — Residency Petition System

In Development L3: Integrate   UMBC • Jason Paluck • Tools: AWS Bedrock, Textract, DynamoDB

Self-service AI system that extracts and analyzes data from uploaded documents (lease agreements, driver's licenses, utility bills) to process in-state tuition residency petitions. Administrative interfaces present summarized application data with recommendations based on state policy.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-10 (Registration & Enrollment) Tier 2
  • Secondary: SS-03 (Financial Aid Triage) Tier 3

Why this mapping: Residency classification directly determines tuition rates—a financial aid determination with major equity implications. The AI analyzes personal identity documents and makes recommendations that affect affordability. This maps to SS-10 for enrollment processing, but the financial impact triggers SS-03 governance requirements.

Coverage: Partially covered — Navigator addresses enrollment and financial aid separately but has no explicit guidance for document-analysis-based tuition classification.

Key governance adds: P2 (equity—residency denials disproportionately affect immigrant and first-gen students), P5 (data handling of identity documents), P6 (transparency of classification logic), NG-03 (must not auto-deny without human review).

#25 — AI Degree Plan Builder

In Development L1: Explore   University of New Mexico • Elisha Allen • Tools: AWS, Python, LLM

Dynamically generates multi-year degree plans based on a student's academic and career interests, major/minor selections, completed credits, and FT/PT status. Supports advisors in generating personalized plans that optimize time to degree.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-01 (AI Advising / Guided Pathways) Tier 3
  • Secondary: SS-09 (Student Journey Mapping) Tier 2

Why this mapping: This is the paradigmatic SS-01 use case—AI generating academic pathways that directly shape a student's degree trajectory. An incorrect or biased plan could extend time-to-degree, increase costs, or narrow career options. Journey mapping applies because the tool considers the full multi-year arc.

Coverage: Fully covered by SS-01.

Key governance adds: P2 (equity—does the optimizer disadvantage part-time or working students?), P4 (student agency—can students override AI suggestions?), P5 (what student data feeds the model?).

#26 — (New) Student Helper

Just an Idea   University of New Mexico • Baset Haridy • Tools: Google NotebookLM

Chatbot for new students to ask questions about campus resources: housing, transportation, food, directions to classrooms. Also intended for new faculty.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1
  • Secondary: SS-07 (Basic Needs Support) Tier 1

Why this mapping: Informational chatbot serving basic campus navigation and resource discovery. Squarely within SS-04's scope. The focus on housing, food, and transportation touches basic needs (SS-07), though in an informational-only capacity.

Coverage: Fully covered by SS-04 + SS-07.

Key governance adds: P1 (institutional purpose—straightforward), P3 (human fallback for complex housing/food insecurity situations), P4 (student agency preserved—informational only).

#41 — Chatbot for Course Registration (AITF 54)

Just an Idea L1: Explore   University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee • Scott Genung • Tools: TBD

Help students find classes, suggest classes, and sign up for individual classes to improve student experience and free up advisors' time.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-10 (Registration & Enrollment) Tier 2
  • Secondary: SS-01 (AI Advising / Guided Pathways) Tier 3

Why this mapping: Combines registration mechanics (finding, enrolling) with advisory functions (suggesting courses). The registration component is SS-10; the suggestion/recommendation component is SS-01. If AI suggests courses, that's an advising function with Tier 3 governance requirements—suggestions can constrain perceived options.

Coverage: Fully covered via SS-10 + SS-01. Multi-function platform guidance (Handbook Gap 3) applies: assess at highest-risk function.

Key governance adds: P2 (do suggestions reflect bias in course availability or section scheduling?), P4 (can students override suggestions and browse freely?), P3 (human advisor accessible if AI suggestions seem wrong).

#42 — Automated Transcript Scanning & Summarization (AITF 32)

Just an Idea L1: Explore   University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee • Scott Genung • Tools: TBD

Reduce time to assess high school transcripts for admissions, enabling faster response to students while reducing labor. Looking at Michigan's TTS model.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-10 (Registration & Enrollment) Tier 2

Why this mapping: Admissions transcript evaluation is an enrollment-pipeline function. AI is accelerating document review, not making admit/deny decisions autonomously, but its summarization shapes the information admissions staff see. Errors could delay or derail a student's admission.

Coverage: Partially covered — SS-10 covers enrollment broadly, but transcript evaluation introduces document-analysis governance concerns (accuracy of credit recognition, handling non-standard transcripts, international credentials) not explicitly addressed.

Key governance adds: P2 (equity—non-traditional transcripts, GED, international credentials), P5 (student academic records as sensitive data), P6 (transparency—can students see what the AI extracted?), NG-03 (AI must not auto-reject based on transcript analysis alone).

#43 — Chatbot for Navigation of UWM Services (AITF 16)

Just an Idea L1: Explore   University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee • Scott Genung • Tools: TBD

Reduces time to find campus content, assists with questions, guides actions, and resolves problems for students, faculty, and staff. Reduces email and call volume.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1
  • Secondary: SS-07 (Basic Needs Support) Tier 1

Why this mapping: General campus services navigation chatbot. Standard SS-04 use case with informational routing. If it "guides actions and resolves problems," it may take actions beyond information delivery—monitor for tier escalation.

Coverage: Fully covered by SS-04.

Key governance adds: P1 (purpose clarity), P3 (human fallback for complex service requests), P4 (student can always reach a human).

#12 — RamGPT

In Development L3: Integrate   Colorado State University • David Edwards • Tools: Cloudforce NebulaOne, Azure, MCP servers

Student-focused chatbot agent making calls to other specialized agents for housing, dining, financial aid, Rec Center, health, campus events, etc. Aspirational goal: match UMich's "Go Blue" system.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1 with tier override
  • Secondary: SS-07 (Basic Needs) Tier 1, SS-03 (Financial Aid) Tier 3

Why this mapping: A multi-agent student services platform. The chatbot interface is SS-04, but its agent-to-agent architecture means it touches financial aid (SS-03, Tier 3), health services, and housing—each with different governance requirements. This is a clear instance of Handbook Gap 3 (multi-function platform): the tool's governance floor equals its highest-risk function.

Coverage: Partially covered — Individual functions are covered by SS-04/SS-07/SS-03, but the multi-agent architecture (AI calling AI) raises governance questions not addressed in the current framework: which agent is accountable? How are inter-agent decisions audited?

Key governance adds: P2 (financial aid equity), P3 (human override at each agent boundary), P5 (data sharing across agents), P6 (transparency—student should know they're interacting with an agent chain), P7 (accountability for multi-agent outputs).

Group B: Institutional AI Platforms (Multi-Function)

#11 — CSU-GPT

In Production L2: Share   Colorado State University • David Edwards • Tools: Cloudforce NebulaOne, Azure, GPT-4o

Secure institutional LLM chat client for all students, faculty, and staff. Supports user-created and shared agents, including brand-compliant writing, social media posts, and course-specific Tutorbots.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1
  • Secondary: SS-05 (AI Course Tutoring) Tier 2 for Tutorbot functions

Why this mapping: Institutional LLM platform that is primarily a general chatbot (SS-04 Tier 1), but the Tutorbot capability elevates specific uses to SS-05 Tier 2. This is a platform-level deployment where the governance tier depends on how students use it.

Coverage: Fully covered via SS-04 + SS-05. Multi-function platform guidance applies.

Key governance adds: P1 (purpose varies by agent), P4 (student/faculty agency in creating agents), P5 (what data do user-created agents access?).

#19 — Purple: AI for All

In Production L3: Integrate   University of Washington • Jared Reimer • Tools: Azure AI Foundry, NebulaOne, GPT-4.1/GPT-5

Agentic AI platform for all UW community members (except possibly UW Medicine).

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1

Why this mapping: Enterprise agentic AI platform. For students, this functions as an SS-04 chatbot, but the "agentic" label suggests it can take actions on behalf of users—a capability that may require tier elevation depending on what actions are delegated. At scale (all of UW), governance must account for diverse use contexts.

Coverage: Fully covered as base chatbot. Agentic capabilities may require additional governance beyond SS-04's scope.

Key governance adds: P3 (human oversight for agentic actions), P4 (student consent for actions taken on their behalf), P5 (data scope of agentic access).

#28 — Help Desk

Just an Idea L5: Evolve   Juniata College • Misty Andrade • Tools: TBD

AI-powered help desk that answers easy questions and takes actions in systems to complete tasks on behalf of students, faculty, and staff. Frees human staff for complex tasks.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1 with tier override to Tier 2

Why this mapping: Starts as SS-04 FAQ chatbot, but the aspiration to "take actions in systems" makes this an agentic system—AI modifying student records, resetting accounts, or processing requests. Action-taking AI requires Tier 2+ governance due to irreversibility and accountability concerns.

Coverage: Partially covered — SS-04 covers FAQ chatbots but does not explicitly address AI that takes system actions on behalf of students.

Key governance adds: P3 (human approval before irreversible actions), P5 (system access scope), P6 (transparency—student sees what actions were taken), P7 (audit trail for system modifications).

#47 — myUMBC Answers

In Production L1: Explore   UMBC • B. Collier Jones • Tools: AWS Bedrock, Custom Integrations

AI-powered search engine of university content with access to personal data to provide personalized answers for students, faculty, and staff.

Navigator Mapping

  • Primary: SS-04 (Student-Facing Chatbot) Tier 1 with tier override to Tier 2

Why this mapping: The personalized data access distinguishes this from a standard SS-04 chatbot. When AI accesses student records to personalize responses, it crosses from informational (Tier 1) into data-driven personalization (Tier 2), triggering P5 (data handling) and P6 (transparency) requirements.

Coverage: Partially covered — SS-04 as base; personal data integration requires additional governance the current SS-04 rubric may not fully address.

Key governance adds: P5 (FERPA—what personal data is accessed?), P6 (can students see what data informed the response?), P4 (can students opt out of personalization?).

5. Gap Analysis

5a. Gaps Confirmed from Handbook Crosswalk

Gap Original Evidence (Handbook) New Evidence (ND Forum) Recommendation
Gap 2: Accessibility & Assistive AI #49 Grammar Lab (RIT), #50 UDL (Cornell), #52 Assistive Tech #13 UGA digital accessibility, #27 Juniata accessibility crawler, #44 Ohio State PDF accessibility (in production) Add SS-12. Now 6 evidence points across 2 sources. OSU's production deployment for Title II makes this urgent.
Gap 3: Multi-Function Platform Guidance #21 Pounce (4 functions), #24 Hawaii (2 functions) #11 CSU-GPT (chatbot + tutoring), #12 RamGPT (chatbot + financial aid + housing + health), #19 Purple (agentic platform) Add portfolio evaluation guidance. Multi-function platforms are the dominant pattern in both sources. The ND Forum data shows this is accelerating with agent-to-agent architectures.

5b. New Gaps Identified

Gap Evidence Problem Recommendation
Gap 6: Agentic AI / AI-Takes-Actions #12 RamGPT (agent-to-agent calls), #19 Purple (agentic AI), #28 Help Desk (takes actions in systems), #47 myUMBC (personalized data access) Current Navigator assumes AI informs humans who then act. Multiple ND Forum use cases describe AI that takes actions in institutional systems—enrolling, resetting, modifying records. SS-04 governance for a chatbot that answers questions is insufficient for a chatbot that executes transactions. Add agentic AI screening question to SS-04 and SS-10: "Does this AI system take actions in institutional systems on behalf of students (e.g., enrolling, submitting, modifying records)?" If yes, auto-upgrade to Tier 2 minimum and add P3 (human approval for irreversible actions), P5 (system access audit), P7 (action audit trail).
Gap 7: AI Document Analysis for Eligibility Determinations #8 UMBC residency petition (analyzes identity documents), #42 UWM transcript scanning (evaluates HS transcripts) AI analyzing personal documents (IDs, leases, transcripts) to make eligibility recommendations (tuition rate, admission). This combines sensitive document handling with consequential decision-making. Neither SS-03 nor SS-10 explicitly address document-analysis-based determination workflows. Add screening question to SS-03 and SS-10: "Does this AI analyze personal documents (transcripts, identity documents, financial records) to determine student eligibility for programs, tuition rates, or admissions?" If yes, add P2 (equity review for document bias), P5 (document retention/destruction policy), NG-03 (no auto-deny).

5c. Gaps Not Confirmed (No New Evidence)

Gap 1 (Mental Health AI): No ND Forum use cases involve mental health AI (WoeBot, Wysa, etc.). This gap remains supported only by Handbook evidence. Recommendation to add SS-11 stands but is not strengthened by this source.

Gap 4 (Yield Optimization): No ND Forum use cases involve enrollment yield prediction. Recommendation for SS-03 screening question stands but is not strengthened.

Gap 5 (Emotional Support Ambiguity): No ND Forum use cases describe emotional support chatbots. SS-04 screening question recommendation stands but is not strengthened.

Key insight: The ND Forum data reveals an emerging pattern not visible in the Handbook: the shift from AI that informs to AI that acts. RamGPT, Purple, Help Desk, and myUMBC Answers all describe agentic capabilities where AI modifies institutional systems or personalizes using student records. The Navigator's current governance model assumes a human decision-maker between AI output and institutional action. Gap 6 (Agentic AI) is the most significant new finding from this crosswalk.
