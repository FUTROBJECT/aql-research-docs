# Navigator v3.1 Content Agent — Progress

## Voice Analysis

### Step 1: v2 Content JSON Patterns

**Landing copy:** Direct, practitioner-facing. "Navigate AI governance. Built for Student Services." — declarative, no filler. Stat strip uses concrete numbers (224+, 14, 10, 9, 3). Credibility statement leads with "so you don't have to."

**Use case profiles:** One-liners are punchy descriptions of what the tool does, not what the pillar evaluates. Screening questions are conversational — "Will a human counselor review AI pathway recommendations before they reach students?" not "Is human-in-the-loop review implemented for recommendation outputs?"

**Pillar copy:** Principle statements are authoritative declarations. Hooks are sharp, memorable lines that anchor the pillar's purpose. Questions use "you" and "your" — addressing the reader directly. "What you're looking for" text legitimizes every answer without judgment.

**No-go criteria:** "Why" text is blunt and values-driven. "What to do" text is specific and actionable. No hedging.

**Scoring rubrics:** Three tiers use plain language. Score 3 is aspirational but achievable. Score 2 is honest about gaps. Score 1 is the red flag — stated without softening.

**Voice signature:** WARM AND BLUNT. A smart colleague who did the research. Uses "you" not "one." Uses dashes for emphasis. Short sentences for impact. Longer sentences for nuance. Never academic. Never bureaucratic.

### Step 2: Wireframe Conversation Analysis

**AI introduces itself:** Functionally, not personally. "I'm here to help you identify and understand the AI tool you're evaluating."

**Tool-specific responses:** Reference the actual product by name. "Mainstay markets itself as a 'chatbot,' but its core mechanism is proactive behavioral messaging." Uses bold for key distinctions.

**Pre-fill phrasing:** "I've pre-filled answers I'm confident about based on Mainstay's published information. Look for the teal-highlighted options." — explains what happened, not just that it happened.

**Uncertainty handling:** "I left Question 3 blank — I don't have reliable information about whether Mainstay's messages are template-based or AI-generated in your specific configuration." — names the specific gap, doesn't generalize.

**Governance deferral:** "Ask your vendor: are we getting the base product or the Behavioral Intelligence tier? The governance requirements are different." — redirects to vendor, explains why it matters.

**Caveat structure:** Every response ends with either (a) a verification prompt, (b) an actionable next step, or (c) a redirect to the vendor. Never just trails off.

**Length:** 2-4 paragraphs. Specific. Uses bullets for lists. Uses bold for emphasis.

### Step 3: v3.1 Voice Synthesis

**Two voices coexist:**

1. **Framework voice** — the institution's voice. WARM AND BLUNT. Direct, practitioner-facing, sharp anchor lines. Uses "you" and "your." Declares, doesn't suggest. Hook lines are memorable.

2. **AI voice** — a knowledgeable colleague lending perception, not judgment. Uses "I" not "we." Confident when factual, honest when uncertain. Never makes governance judgments. Always ends with actionable next step. References tools by name. Uses caveats structurally, not defensively.

**Both voices serve the same user.** A VP of Student Services or Dean evaluating an AI tool for their community college. Busy. Smart. Slightly overwhelmed by AI governance. Needs to get to a defensible answer.

**Key v3.1 additions over v2:**
- Routing questions use same conversational voice as screening
- Dependency profile questions feel like a colleague asking practical questions
- Mechanism detection context notes are plain-language, not jargon
- AI conversation content is the most important new content — must feel real
